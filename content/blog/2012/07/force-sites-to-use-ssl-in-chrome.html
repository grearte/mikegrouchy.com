--- title: Force websites to use SSL in Google Chrome author: Mike Grouchy created: !!timestamp 2012-07-23 9:30:00 tags: - browser - security - tip --- I saw this tip in the comments over at [Hacker News](http://news.ycombinator.com) today and I thought that this is a great alternative to force SSL for sites that don't have it available. ##Step 1 Navigate to Chrome's net internals dashboard by typing `chrome://net-internals/` into your browser. You should see: ![net-internals](/media/images/2012/07/net-internals.png) ##Step 2 Navigate to the [HSTS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HTTP_Strict_Transport_Security)(HTTPS Strict Transport Security) Tab and you should see this: ![hsts](/media/images/2012/07/hsts.png) ##Step 3 From there its pretty easy, just type in the site you want to force to SSL and you are done! ![hsts](/media/images/2012/07/add-hsts-domain.png) Do you have any other Chrome security tips? Leave them in the comments!
